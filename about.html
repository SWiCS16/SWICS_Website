ABOUT

Who We Are

  Stevens Women in Computer Science (SWiCS) Club aims to unite undergraduate students of all genders, majors, enthinicties and other identities in order to promote women in the computer science related fields obth in academia and industry.

  Situated at Stevens Institute of Technology - a premiere private research university in Hoboken, NJ, SWiCS flourishes in an environment where 30% of the population is female and out of all computer science majors, less than 45% are female.

  Against all odds, SWiCS is the driving force that establishes computer science as a highly desirable career for women through an exciting array of workshops, networking opportunities, and conferences.

Purpose

  The purpose of this club is to bring together students who support the advancement of women in Computer Science in both academia and in industry. We provide support for women pursuing a Computer Science major, and encourage all other students who have interest in Computer Science. We collaborate with other WiCS chapters around the country through conferences and networking events to encourage the involvement of women in S.T.E.M and provide career development. Within Stevens and our Hoboken community, we promote programming and technical skills to women through campus-wide workshops that introduce computer science topics to beginners as well as continue to improve the skills of advanced computer scientists.

What We've Acheived

  • Created a community where women could find like-minded others
  • Grew to an organization of 100+ members on our mailing list and 40+ members attending events
  • Sponsored students to Grace Hopper Conference, funding 10 women in 2018
  • Sponsored students to Harvard WECode, funding 12 women in 2017
  • Have roughly 10 events per semester to provide programming at all levels
  • Offer technical workshops to supplement CS coursework
  • Hold Coffee&&Code sessions with BWE Kafe and Jefferson Coffee
